Predict Your High School Success

Answer the following questions to predict your high school success.

1. What is your sex?


2. What is your race/ethnicity?


3. Is English your native language?


4. Did at least one of your parents graduate high school?


5. Please estimate your family income.


6. Is your school located in a rural area?


7. What region of the United States is your school located?


8. Do any of the following statements apply to you?

  • You come from a single-parent household.
  • Both of your parents do not a high school diploma.
  • You have a sibling who has dropped out of school.
  • You have changed schools two or more times (excluding changes due to school promotions).
  • You have previously repeated at least one grade.
  • You come from a household with an income below the federal threshold for poverty. Households are considered impoverished if family income is:
    $20,000 or less and household size is 5 or less;
    $25,000 or less if household size is 6 or 7;
    $35,000 or less if household size is 8; and
    $50,000 or less if household size is 9 or more.


9. Overall, about how much time do you spend on homework each week while at school?


10. Overall, about how much time do you spend on homework each week outside of school?


11. Did you work during the school year?


12. In a typical week, how much time do you spend on school-sponsored extracurricular activities (e.g., sports, school clubs)?


13. How much additional reading do you do each week on your own outside of school? Do not count any school-assigned reading.


14. On average, how many hours per weekday do you spend watching TV/videos or playing video games?


15. How many hours a day do you usually use a computer other than for schoolwork?


16. How often do you spend time volunteering or performing community service outside of school?


17. Overall, the teaching at your school is good. Agree or disagree?


18. Because doing mathematics is fun, I wouldn't want to give it up. Agree or disagree?


19. Other students often disrupt class. Agree or disagree?


20. Have you been recognized for good grades (e.g., honor roll)?


21. How important are good grades to you?


22. You go to school because your parents expect you to succeed. Agree or disagree?


23. Among your close friends, how important is it to them that they get good grades?


24. How often do you come to class without a pencil/pen or paper?


25. You don't feel safe at your school. Agree or disagree?


26. How many times were you absent from school during the first semester or term of this school year?


27. How many times did you get in trouble for not following school rules during the first semester or term of this school year?


28. How important is getting away from this area of the country to you in your life?


29. Does your family have access to the internet at your home?


30. Do you have your own room at your home?


31. How often do your parents check whether you have done your homework?
